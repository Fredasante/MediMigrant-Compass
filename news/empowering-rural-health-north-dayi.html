Empowering Rural Health: First Aid Volunteer Training in North Dayi

Since 2019, MediMigrant Compass e.V Deutschland, in partnership with EuroAfric Therapy Tour – Deutschland, has been actively supporting underserved communities in Ghana’s North Dayi Constituency through targeted healthcare interventions. Led by Solace Yaa Gawu, the team has consistently traveled to remote villages, often navigating poor roads and limited infrastructure, to monitor progress, donate medical equipment, and offer hands-on support. In early 2024, the organizations launched a vital initiative: sponsoring and training First Aid Volunteers across deep rural areas of North Dayi.

This program was born out of necessity. Many of these villages lack access to medical doctors, face frequent nighttime emergencies, and suffer preventable deaths due to delayed care. With no nearby clinics and limited transportation, basic first aid knowledge can mean the difference between life and loss. The training equips local volunteers with life-saving skills to respond to injuries, sudden illnesses, and emergencies—bridging the gap until professional help arrives. It’s a grassroots solution to a systemic challenge, rooted in compassion and community resilience.

MediMigrant Compass e.V is a Germany-based nonprofit focused on migrant health, education, and empowerment. EuroAfric Therapy Tour complements this mission by promoting holistic wellness and intercultural collaboration between Europe and Africa. Together, they believe: Prevention is always better than cure. That’s why they continue to call on individuals, organizations, and donors to support this movement. One urgent need is the donation of digital health monitors—tools that can help track vital signs and prevent silent emergencies.

We invite everyone to join this growing healthcare and preventive movement. Your support can save lives, uplift communities, and build a healthier future—one village at a time
